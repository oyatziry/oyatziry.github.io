Tutorfly Blog Redesign

Tutorfly is a tutoring platform whose mission is to provide an educational alternative to traditional classroom settings through peer-to-peer tutoring.

As the UI/UX intern, I re-evaluated the web experience of our users to improve navigation and create a seamless user interface. One major project of mine was to redesign the Tutorfly blog page to make it more practical and effective.

Research

As a starting company with few resources, a lot of our user research came from analyzing any key performance indicators through Google Analytics. As a result, checking our data on Google Analytics became one of my bi-weekly tasks during sprint meetings.

In one of my bi-weekly analysis, I found that the blog page had one of the biggest bounce rates. It differed by 19.25% compared to the other pages on the site. This meant that many of our users were landing on the blog page but quickly left the page after viewing it for a brief moment. The Tutofly blog page contains several college readiness resources for our high school students. Finding a high bounce rate for the blog page meant that many of our users (the majority of whom are high school students) were not taking advantage of the resources provided in the blog page.

My initial thought was to think if perhaps the blog page was useless. After conducting a few user surveys, I found that many users found the blog page to be too disorganized.



The Problem

The initial challenge with the blog page

Currently, the blog page is not organized by topics and is simply an infinite scroll page. In addition, the pictures displayed in the blog page are not consistent and appear random.



Redesign


Initially, I was eager to start designing the page upfront. I thought of colors and the visual aesthetic before anything else. I quickly realized that in order to design an effective new blog page, I needed to organize the page first. I began by writing each blog post title on sticky notes to get a visual representation on the number of posts I had to work with. After noticing the large amount of posts I had to work with, I found it best to group posts into categories and started brainstorming category titles. I thought this information structure was best because one of the main issues with the current blog page was lack of organization. By categorizing blog posts, I was able to find a solution to the infinite scroll page.


Categorizing the blog topics through card sorting




Tutorfly Style Guide

Once I organized the blog posts into categories, I then had to think about the visual design while keeping the Tutorfly style guide in mind. One design feature I kept close to mind when redesigning was the flow of the webpage. I wanted to ensure the new design flowed easily through each blog post and everything was easily accessible. Another feature I had in mind was to break the text heaviness blog posts with fun and related images.



Potential transitions for blog

Having all these ideas in mind, I began to sketch the information structure and laid out the organization of the new blog page. I then used Figma to create a high-fidelity wireframe.

Deliverables
